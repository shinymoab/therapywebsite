Home

In these extraordinary times it is normal to feel different, more stressed, quicker to lose your cool and perhaps a bit gloomy about everything. Nevertheless, you might feel that you would like help and support to find your way of coping with so much change.

Therapy helps us understand ourselves, by becoming clearer about who we are, what we feel, what we want and what we might do next. I take pleasure in listening and engaging, embracing fully the truth that we are all complex and changing. With all your trepidation and anticipation embraced, I accompany and welcome your next step in this human journey.

Do you keep going around in circles? Unsure what to do next? Stuck in a never-changing routine, do you feel confused and overwhelmed?

I offer a confidential and safe space to explore how to improve your overall sense of wellbeing and we will look in detail at any areas in your life or your mental health that you would like to work on. I invite you to embark on a journey with me to find out which therapies and tools work for you and make a difference. I would like to accompany you when you discover blocks or hurdles that are new or have been part of your life for a long time. Together we can work at finding out what purpose they serve and if needed working on letting them go.

In the therapy process, resources that are hiding in you and you are not currently able to use, show themselves. We will nourish them to let them develop into active parts of your daily routine.

Methods and Techniques

Person-centred psychotherapy “As no one else can know how we perceive, we are the best experts on ourselves.” (Carl Rogers)

Using person-centred therapy, I hope to help my clients achieve personal growth and eventually to realize your full potential. The aim is to fully develop your abilities and appreciation for life. Of great importance, therefore, is the quality of our relationship.

My role is to facilitate in creating an environment in which you can bring about positive change. I encourage you to take an active role in our therapy sessions. You know what is best for you and I hope to reflect this attitude in our therapy sessions.
